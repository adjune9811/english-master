Grammar

Present simple

Used for habits, facts, and routines. Add -s or -es for he/she/it.

She works every day. I eat breakfast at 8am.

Past simple

Used for completed actions. Regular verbs add -ed. Irregular verbs change form.

He walked to school. They went to the park.

Present perfect

Use have/has + past participle for actions that connect past to now.

I have lived here for five years. She has just arrived.

Future simple

Use will + base verb for predictions and spontaneous decisions.

I will call you tomorrow. It will rain tonight.

A / An (indefinite)

Use before singular countable nouns. A before consonant sounds, an before vowel sounds.

A cat. An apple. An honest man.

The (definite)

Use when both speaker and listener know the specific thing. Also for unique objects.

The sun rises in the east. Close the door.

No article (zero article)

No article with plural/uncountable nouns in general statements, or proper nouns.

Water is essential. Dogs are friendly animals.

In / On / At — time

In for months/years/seasons. On for days/dates. At for specific times.

In January. On Monday. At 9 o'clock.

In / On / At — place

In for enclosed spaces. On for surfaces. At for specific locations.

In the room. On the table. At the station.

For / Since / During

For a duration. Since a start point. During within a period.

I studied for two hours. I have lived here since 2020.

Zero conditional

Facts and general truths. If + present, present.

If you heat water, it boils.

First conditional

Real future possibility. If + present, will + base verb.

If it rains, I will stay home.

Second conditional

Unreal/hypothetical present. If + past, would + base verb.

If I had money, I would travel.

Third conditional

Past unreal. If + past perfect, would have + past participle.

If I had studied, I would have passed.
